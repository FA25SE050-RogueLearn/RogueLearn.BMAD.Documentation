### **UC-02: Guild Community & Engagement**

This use case defines the lifecycle of guild participation, from joining as a member to managing it as a Guild Master.

**Actors:** Guild Member (Student), Guild Master

---

## Transactions (Member Experience)

1.  **Student A Discovers and Joins a Guild**
    *   Following the suggestion from UC-01, Student A navigates to the "Guilds" page.
    *   They browse a list of public guilds and find "FCode - Java Team". The guild requires approval to join.
    *   Student A clicks "Apply to Join" and writes a short message. The request status becomes "Pending".

2.  **Guild Master Reviews and Approves Application**
    *   The Guild Master of "FCode - Java Team" receives a notification.
    *   In the Guild Management panel, they review Student A's profile and application.
    *   The Guild Master clicks "Approve".

3.  **Student A Participates in the Guild**
    *   Student A receives a notification that their application was approved and is now a member.
    *   They access the guild's main page, which features a post feed.
    *   They read a pinned "Welcome" post and a discussion thread about a difficult coding problem.

4.  **Student A Participates in a Post**
    *   Student A views a post the Guild Master created.
    *   They comment on the post, expressing their understanding of the problem.
    *   Other members can also comments on the post or reply to student A comment.

## Transactions (Guild Master Experience)

5.  **Guild Master Manages Members**
    *   The Guild Master views the member list. They see a long-standing, helpful member (Student C).
    *   The Master promotes Student C to the "Officer" role, granting them moderation privileges.
    *   They also notice an inactive member and choose to remove them from the guild.

6.  **Guild Master Configures the Guild**
    *   The Guild Master navigates to the guild settings.
    *   They update the guild's description and upload a new banner image.
    *   They decide to make joining easier and change the privacy setting from "Requires Approval" to "Public".
  
7.  **Guild Master Monitors and Moderates**
    *   The Guild Master regularly checks the post feed for any inappropriate content.
    *   They use the moderation tools to delete or warn users who break the rules.

8.  **Guild Master Creates Meetings**
    *   The Guild Master decides to host a regular meeting.
    *   In the guild settings, they create a new meeting with a specified time and location.
    *   They invite all relevant members to the meeting.
